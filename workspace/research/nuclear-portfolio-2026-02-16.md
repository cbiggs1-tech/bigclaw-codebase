## 4. Key Nuclear Sector Risks

### Regulatory / NRC
- **NRC licensing timeline:** New reactor designs take 5-10+ years for approval. OKLO was denied once already. SMR got design cert but the actual construction/operating license is separate.
- **Political risk:** Nuclear policy can shift with administrations. Current environment is favorable (bipartisan support via ADVANCE Act), but this can change.
- **10 CFR 50 vs 10 CFR 53:** The new Part 53 framework for advanced reactors is still being finalized. Regulatory uncertainty adds years to timelines.

### Construction & Execution
- **Vogtle precedent:** Units 3&4 went from $14B budget to $35B+ and 7 years late. This is the ghost that haunts every new-build proposal.
- **Supply chain:** Nuclear-grade components (ASME Section III) have limited suppliers. BWXT is one of very few. This is both a risk (bottleneck) and an opportunity (moat for BWXT).
- **Workforce:** Qualified nuclear welders, NDE technicians, and I&C engineers are scarce. The industry lost a generation of talent during the post-Fukushima downturn.

### Uranium Supply
- **Spot vs contract pricing:** Uranium spot is elevated but long-term contracts (where utilities actually buy) tell the real story. CCJ is well-positioned but prices can be volatile.
- **Geopolitical:** ~40% of world uranium enrichment is Russian (TENEX/Rosatom). US HALEU supply is a national security issue — this is LEU's bull case.
- **Kazakhstan (Kazatomprom):** World's largest uranium producer. Any supply disruption or policy change moves the entire market.

### Public Sentiment & NIMBY
- **Three Mile Island restart:** CEG's deal with Microsoft to restart TMI Unit 1 is a bellwether. If it succeeds, it legitimizes the nuclear revival. If it stalls, sentiment turns.
- **Waste storage:** Still no permanent repository (Yucca Mountain remains politically dead). Every reactor stores spent fuel on-site in dry casks. Not a near-term business risk but a perpetual public perception issue.

### Valuation Risk
- **The sector has already run.** CCJ is up 50% in 6 months. Many names are priced for perfection.
- **AI data center narrative:** If the AI buildout slows or power purchase agreements don't materialize at expected scale, the premium evaporates quickly.
- **Interest rates:** Nuclear projects are capital-intensive. Higher-for-longer rates increase construction financing costs.
