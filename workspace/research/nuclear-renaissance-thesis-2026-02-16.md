## 1. Executive Summary & Investment Thesis

### Why the Nuclear Renaissance Is Real

The convergence of three megatrends has created the most favorable environment for nuclear energy since the Atoms for Peace era:

**1. AI Data Center Power Demand — The Insatiable Appetite**
Big Tech's combined AI infrastructure spend is projected at **$650 billion in 2026** (Amazon, Alphabet, Meta, Microsoft). Each hyperscale data center demands 500MW–2GW of reliable, 24/7 baseload power — exactly what nuclear provides and what renewables alone cannot. Meta alone just signed deals for **6.6 GW of nuclear capacity** across TerraPower, Oklo, and Vistra. Microsoft's 20-year PPA with Constellation to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1 (now "Crane Clean Energy Center") signals that Big Tech is willing to underwrite nuclear economics directly.

**2. Policy Tailwinds — Executive Order 14300 & NRC Reform**
President Trump's EO 14300 mandates comprehensive NRC reform with **fixed licensing deadlines: 18 months maximum for new-reactor construction-and-operation applications, 12 months for license renewals**. The NRC has launched a major reorganization to comply, including new offices and streamlined review processes. A proposed microreactor licensing rule is targeted for March 2026, with a final rule by September 2026. The ADVANCE Act (bipartisan) further accelerates advanced reactor deployment. This is the most pro-nuclear regulatory environment in decades.

**3. Energy Security & Grid Reliability**
Russian enrichment dependence (~40% of global SWU capacity) has made domestic nuclear fuel a national security imperative. Grid reliability concerns — from Texas winter storms to California rolling blackouts — have rehabilitated nuclear's reputation as the ultimate baseload source. The conversation has shifted from "how do we shut them down?" to "how fast can we build them?"

### The Investment Thesis in One Sentence
**Nuclear is the only energy source that satisfies Big Tech's simultaneous demands for massive baseload capacity, 24/7 reliability, and carbon-free credentials — and the policy environment has never been more supportive.**

### The Catch
Much of this is already priced in. Nuclear stocks have run 50–200%+ in the past year. The thesis is about separating the **real operators generating cash today** from the **speculative plays that won't generate a single watt for 5–10 years**. As someone with 43 years of NRC licensing experience, Curtis, you have a unique edge in evaluating who's real and who's running a PowerPoint reactor.

## 6. Deep Dive: The SMR Race — SMR vs OKLO & the Molten Salt Landscape

### The Licensing Timeline = The Key Differentiator

Curtis's thesis — and it's a good one — is that **NRC licensing timeline is the single most important variable** for which advanced reactor company wins AI data center contracts. Big Tech doesn't care about theoretical reactor superiority. They care about: *When can you deliver megawatts to my data center?*

Here's the licensing landscape as of February 2026:

### NuScale (SMR) — The Regulatory Leader

| Milestone | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| NRC Design Certification | ✅ **Approved** (Standard Design Approval, 2023) — **Only company with this** |
| Construction Permit | ❌ None (Idaho project cancelled 2023) |
| Operating License | ❌ None |
| Flagship Project | ❌ Cancelled — seeking new site/customer |
| Technology | Light-water PWR, natural circulation, 77 MWe modules |
| Estimated First Operation | 2031–2033 (if a new project is signed soon) |

**Assessment:** NuScale has the regulatory crown jewel — actual NRC design certification — but no customer. The design uses proven light-water technology with no coolant pumps (natural circulation), which is a genuine safety innovation Curtis would recognize. But design cert ≠ operating reactor. They need a site, a utility partner, and a construction/operating license. The clock hasn't started on the hard part.

### Oklo — The Silicon Valley Darling

| Milestone | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| NRC Combined License Application | ❌ **Denied (2022)** — insufficient safety analysis. Resubmitted. |
| DOE Pilot Program | ✅ Selected for Reactor Pilot Program at INL |
| Technology | Metallic fuel, sodium-cooled fast reactor (Aurora), 15 MWe |
| HALEU Fuel | Building fuel fabrication facility at INL |
| Key Partnerships | Meta (1.2 GW Ohio plant), DOE fuel recycling grant |
| Estimated First Operation | 2028–2030 (DOE pilot), commercial 2030+ |

**Assessment:** Oklo is pursuing the most ambitious path — combined license (design + construction + operating in one application), fast reactor technology, fuel recycling. The NRC denial was a serious setback. Their resubmission needs to address the deficiencies. The DOE Reactor Pilot Program is a parallel path that bypasses NRC (DOE self-regulates its own sites), which could get a demonstration reactor operating sooner. But the 1.2 GW Ohio commercial plant for Meta? That's NRC jurisdiction, and Oklo has never successfully navigated it.

### The Molten Salt Reactor (MSR) Landscape — Private Companies to Watch

These aren't publicly traded, but they're the competitive context:

#### Natura Resources — Leading the MSR NRC Race
- **100 MW molten salt reactor** design
- **January 2026:** DOE allocated historic FLiBe (lithium-7 fluoride) salt from the original MSRE at Oak Ridge — this is a material advantage no one else has
- **NGL Water Solutions partnership:** Combining MSR power with Permian Basin produced water treatment — brilliant use case (desalination is energy-intensive)
- **NRC Status:** Leading among MSR developers in licensing engagement
- **Curtis's Take:** Natura has the most pragmatic approach — they're not trying to change the world, they're trying to solve a specific industrial problem (produced water) with proven physics

#### Kairos Power — The TRISO-Salt Hybrid
- **Hermes test reactor** in Oak Ridge, TN — **NRC construction permit approved** (first non-water-cooled reactor approved in 50 years)
- **14 NRC topical reports approved** as of January 2026 — building the licensing case methodically
- **Technology:** TRISO fuel particles in fluoride salt coolant (FLiBe) — not a true MSR (fuel is solid TRISO, not dissolved in salt), but gets many MSR benefits (low pressure, high thermal efficiency)
- **Google partnership** for power supply
- **BWXT makes their TRISO fuel** — another reason to own BWXT
- **Estimated first criticality:** Late 2026–2027 for Hermes test reactor

#### Terrestrial Energy — IMSR (Integral Molten Salt Reactor)
- **Canadian company** with NRC pre-application engagement
- **Mid-stage licensing** — further behind Natura and Kairos
- **IMSR design:** True molten salt reactor with fuel dissolved in fluoride salt
- **Timeline:** Several years behind the leaders

#### TerraPower — The Gates-Backed Natrium
- **Not actually MSR** — Natrium is a sodium-cooled fast reactor with molten salt energy storage
- **Meta deal:** Up to 8 reactors, 1.2 GW, online potentially by 2030
- **Kemmerer, WY:** Construction underway on first commercial Natrium reactor
- **NRC accelerated review:** EO 14300 + DOE support
- **Private company** (Bill Gates-backed) — not directly investable

#### ACU MSRR — The Research Reactor
- **Abilene Christian University's Molten Salt Research Reactor**
- **NRC construction permit under review** — research reactor, not commercial
- **Expected deployment:** Later 2026
- **Significance:** First university MSR, validates molten salt licensing pathway
