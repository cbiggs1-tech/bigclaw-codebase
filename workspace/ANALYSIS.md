# ANALYSIS.md - Post-Data Synthesis Protocol

## When to Apply
Use this protocol whenever you've gathered data from multiple tools (scripts, web search, X, news) for any analytical query — stock analysis, portfolio review, market outlook, or custom research.

## The Synthesis Loop
After collecting data, STOP before formatting output. Run this mental loop:

### 1. Identify Core Narratives (2–3 max)
What are the dominant forces at play? Examples:
- Macro/geopolitical drag vs company-specific catalyst
- Technical breakdown vs fundamental strength
- Sentiment divergence (retail bullish, institutions selling)

### 2. Cross-Analyze Interactions
How do these narratives interact? Which dominates near-term (1–3 months) vs long-term (6–12 months)?
- Does the macro story override the stock-specific thesis, or vice versa?
- Are the signals confirming each other or contradicting?
- What's the one thing that could flip the dominant narrative?

### 3. Apply to Curtis's Position & Rules
- Does this trigger any portfolio thresholds (decision engine scores, bond score overrides, stop losses)?
- What would change conviction? Be specific about the data point or price level.
- How does this interact with Curtis's existing positions or cash allocation?

### 4. Output: Insight First, Data Second
Structure the response as synthesis with embedded evidence, NOT a data dump with a conclusion tacked on.

**Bad:** "X sentiment: 62% bullish. RSI: 38. 50-day MA crossed below 200-day. Oil at $87. Conclusion: mixed signals."

**Good:** "TSLA's caught between oversold technicals (RSI 38, usually a bounce zone) and a macro headwind that most people are misreading. Oil at $87 on Iran fears — counterintuitively bearish for EVs because the market treats it as risk-off for growth names, even though high oil should help the EV adoption thesis long-term. The death cross (50-day below 200-day) looks scary but has a 25% false-positive rate for TSLA specifically. I lean toward a bounce to $360 before any real resolution, with April deliveries as the make-or-break catalyst."

## Key Phrases to Use
- "The key tension is..."
- "This implies..."
- "Net effect on thesis..."
- "I lean toward... because..."
- "What would change my mind..."
- "The market is pricing X but missing Y..."
- "Conviction: [1-10] because..."

## What NOT to Do
- Don't lead with raw numbers — lead with what they mean
- Don't list 10 bullet points of data without connecting them
- Don't say "mixed signals" and leave it there — say which side you lean toward and why
- Don't end with "monitor closely" — end with specific levels, dates, or triggers
- Don't repeat the same fact in different sections — say it once where it matters most
